# Concepts

## Used

- The player controls a satellite that orbits planets.
- Pressing Space or tapping on mobile launches the satellite outward from its current planet.
- The satellite can orbit the sun when it reaches the sun's yellow orbit ring.
- The satellite can be captured by another planet when it reaches that planet's capture orbit.
- Planet orbits get farther apart as the player moves outward.
- Outer orbits contain more planets, scaled by orbit radius, to reduce waiting for alignment.
- Planet speeds alternate between about double speed and normal speed by orbit, then slow down farther from the sun.
- Planet sizes vary more, and larger planets have larger capture orbit rings.
- The satellite has an energy blade that can hit asteroids while orbiting or jumping.
- The energy blade length tracks ship health.
- Slicing asteroids restores a small amount of ship health, which lengthens the blade.
- Rare spinning pentagon blade upgrade rings appear around selected planets and replenish after collection.
- Powerup rings do not appear on the planet the ship is currently attached to.
- Entering a planet orbit with a blade upgrade consumes it and temporarily makes the blade super long and thick.
- The ship and blade orbit faster while the super blade is active.
- Destroyed asteroids add to the asteroid score.
- A dense asteroid field can break into smaller fragments.
- Asteroid and enemy scores reset when the ship dies.
- Red cube sentries are fixed between planet orbits, starting from the third ring onward.
- Each red cube sentry has a large red firing radius.
- Entering a sentry radius makes that sentry slowly follow the ship until destroyed.
- Enemy sizes vary widely, and larger enemies have larger red firing rings.
- Cube sentries fire whenever the player is inside their red radius.
- While inside an enemy red radius, the ship cannot attach to planet or sun orbit rings.
- The blade can slice and destroy red cube sentries.
- Enemy blade hitboxes are spherical and larger than the visible cube for clearer hits.
- Destroyed sentries add to the enemy score.
- Destroyed red cube sentries respawn after a delay.
- Sentry lasers lock onto the ship as flashing beam lines, damage the ship, and update the health bar.
- The ship flashes white when damaged.
- The ship explodes only after its health reaches zero, then returns to the starting planet.
- The outermost yellow ring turns the ship back toward the sun and launches it inward.
- Hitting the outermost yellow ring gives the ship a quick turn-around flip.
- The camera locks onto the current orbit body while the ship is orbiting, then follows the ship during free flight.
